# Changelog

All notable changes to Cella are documented here. The format is based on [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/), and the project aims to follow [Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/).

## [Unreleased]

### Added

- **Standalone Go core.** Cella is now a single, self-contained binary — no WordPress, no database server, no CGO. Local persistence is SQLite via a pure-Go driver.
- **Governance-action ingest** (`cella ingest`) — pull on-chain governance actions and Constitutional Committee votes from Koios, with CIP-108 anchor metadata parsed for titles and abstracts.
- **AI-assisted constitutionality review** (`cella review`) — assess stored actions against the Cardano Constitution using any OpenAI-compatible model (bring your own: OpenAI, OpenRouter, Groq, vLLM, LM Studio, local Ollama). The review is grounded in the actual Constitution text, not the model's recollection of it.
- **Embedded Constitution** — the versioned Constitution (v0 interim, v1, v2.4 current) ships inside the binary and is browsable with revision switching.
- **Web UI** (`cella serve`) — governance actions dashboard and per-action detail pages: metadata, abstract, the full Constitutional Committee vote roster with rationales, and the AI review.
- **Entry chamber** — an entry splash and session gate fronting the private chamber.
- **Wallet sign-in** — real CIP-30 authentication: the browser signs a server-issued challenge (CIP-8 / COSE_Sign1) and the server verifies the Ed25519 signature. No key ever leaves the wallet.
- **The on-chain payload.** A delegate can now see what an action actually *does*, not just what its authors said about it: every recipient of a treasury withdrawal with exact amounts and shares, the protocol parameters a change would set, the version a hard fork targets, the seats a committee update adds and removes, the document a new constitution would install. Amounts are arbitrary-precision — a treasury figure that has been through a float64 is a figure that may be wrong.
- **Lifecycle status.** Actions are badged Live / Ratified / Enacted / Dropped / Expired from the chain's own epochs. A settled action no longer runs a countdown, and sinks below the live ones.
- **How the rest of the chain is voting.** Stake-weighted DRep and SPO tallies from `/proposal_voting_summary`, shown as context for the committee's own decision. Vote counts and stake weight can diverge sharply, and a committee cannot see that divergence in a headcount.
- **The committee and its threshold, from the chain.** `/committee_info` gives the seats, who has resigned, when terms end, and the quorum fraction. With 7 authorized seats and a 2/3 quorum the committee needs **5** Yes votes to ratify, not 4 — Cella now states the threshold and whether it has been met.
- **The proposer's case** — CIP-108 motivation and the proposer's own rationale, alongside the deposit, the return address, and `meta_is_valid`. When the anchored metadata does not hash to what was committed on-chain, Cella says so: the abstract a delegate is reading may not be the one the proposer signed for.
- **A navigable Constitution.** Every article and section has an anchor and a permalink; there is a sticky table of contents that tracks your position, and an in-page search with match navigation. Governance actions deep-link into the articles that govern them. The table of contents collects level-2 *and* level-3 headings, because the interim revision writes its articles at level 3 — a table of contents that gathered only level 2 would be empty on v0.
- **Chamber flags and private notes.** Delegates raise *needs discussion* / *ready* / *blocked* to their colleagues — each delegate's flag stands on its own, so nobody can silently lower a colleague's. Private notes autosave, are never published, and cannot be read by anyone else.
- **Delegates sign their positions.** A recorded vote used to be only as trustworthy as the session cookie that recorded it. A delegate now signs their position with their wallet, over a plain-language message the wallet displays in full — the body, the action, the vote and the rationale are all inside the signature. The server composes that message and rebuilds it independently to verify, so nothing the browser claims was signed is trusted: a signature cannot be lifted onto a different action, altered after the fact, or used to record a position in another delegate's name.
- **Committee rationale (CIP-136).** Author the committee's citable rationale and emit it as a real CIP-136 JSON-LD document at `/rationale/{action}.jsonld`. Cella computes and displays the document's **anchor hash** — the blake2b-256 of the exact served bytes, the same value `cardano-cli hash anchor-data --file-text` prints, and the one submitted on-chain with the vote. The `internalVote` block is derived from the chamber's own deliberation, and the delegates who recorded a position are named as authors. A rationale CIP-136 would reject cannot be downloaded, because an anchor hash over an incomplete document looks submittable when it is not.
- **Quorum comes from the chain.** Cella reads the hot NFT's inline datum (`CELLA_HOT_NFT_ADDR`) to learn who may actually sign the committee's vote, and shows quorum against that — never against its own roster. The rule is the validator's: **ceil(n/2)** distinct voting keys, so a group of four needs two signatures, not three. Only *signed* positions count towards it, because an unsigned one is a database row and not a signature the chain will accept. With no datum read, Cella reports quorum unknown rather than guessing.
- **Deadline countdowns.** Governance actions expire, and one that expires unvoted is an abstention the committee never chose — so the dashboard now leads with the clock and quorum progress, sorted with the most urgent first and expired actions sunk to the bottom. Urgency is colour-coded and the countdown ticks live, so a page left open does not go quietly stale.
- **On-chain submission flow** — walks the credential-manager / hot-NFT multisig path (anchor, compose, build, co-sign, submit), gated on having an anchorable rationale.
- Initial public repository: README, governance docs, SECURITY policy, Apache-2.0 LICENSE, CI workflow, and Dependabot configuration.

### Fixed
- **The deadline was a full epoch too generous.** Voting closes at the *start* of an action's expiration epoch, not the end of it — for every expired action Koios reports, `expired_epoch` equals `expiration` exactly. Cella read it as the end, handing every committee five days of mainnet runway that did not exist, on the one number they cannot afford to be wrong about.

- **Every action claimed to have expired in 1970.** The chain states an action's expiration as an epoch *number*, but Cella formatted it as a Unix timestamp — so `epoch 648` rendered as `1970-01-01`, and the dashboard showed no expiry at all. Cella now captures the network's genesis parameters at ingest (Koios `/genesis`) and derives a real wall-clock deadline: the end of the expiration epoch, since an action stays votable through it. The arithmetic is pinned in tests against Koios's own slot numbers, and where the genesis parameters are unavailable Cella shows the raw epoch and no countdown rather than inventing a date.
- **The submission flow named the wrong keys.** It told delegates to co-sign with their *cold* keys. The cold credential authorizes and rotates the hot credential and resigns the seat; it does not cast votes. Votes are signed with the **voting keys** that satisfy the hot NFT script. Wrong guidance about which keys to bring out of cold storage is the worst kind of wrong.

### Changed

- **The chamber shows only real positions.** Member stances were previously generated deterministically from the proposal id to illustrate the flow. They are gone: the chamber shows exactly what delegates have recorded, a delegate who has not voted is shown as awaiting rather than given an invented opinion, and a tie abstains rather than being resolved into a mandate the body never reached.
- **The delegate roster is configuration** (`CELLA_ROSTER`), not a hardcoded struct of fictional delegates. It carries each delegate's wallet address (how Cella recognises them) and their on-chain voting key hash. A roster whose address cannot be decoded fails at startup rather than at sign-in. The fallback roster's addresses are deliberately not valid bech32, so an unconfigured deployment cannot accidentally authenticate anyone.
- CI takes its Go version from `go.mod`, so the two cannot drift apart.

### Security

- **Roster sign-in is off by default**, behind `CELLA_DEMO`. The entry splash's member picker signs a visitor in as whichever delegate they click, with no proof of identity — and it was reachable on every deployment, so anyone who could load the page could vote as any delegate and author the committee's rationale. It is now refused at the endpoint (not merely hidden in the page, which would leave a direct post working), and both the splash and the server log say plainly what demo mode gives away.
- **Wallet sign-in identifies the actual delegate.** `/auth/verify` verified any wallet's signature correctly and then signed the holder in as a hardcoded name — so every delegate who connected a wallet became the same person, and any stranger with any Cardano wallet could walk in as a delegate. Cella now identifies the signer from the key inside the signature (blake2b-224 of the verified Ed25519 public key) and matches that credential against the roster. The address the browser sends is ignored entirely, since trusting it would let a stranger sign with their own key while claiming a delegate's address.
- **Session cookies are signed** (HMAC-SHA256, keyed by `CELLA_SECRET`). The cookie previously carried a delegate's identity in the clear, so anyone could set it to any name on the roster and record votes as that delegate. An unverifiable cookie is now treated as no session at all, and the cookie is marked `Secure` whenever the request arrived over HTTPS.
- **Vote and rationale posts require a CSRF token** bound to the session identity, so a token minted for one delegate cannot authorize a post as another.
- **Security headers** on every response: `X-Frame-Options: DENY` and a strict `Content-Security-Policy` (including `frame-ancestors 'none'` and `form-action 'self'`), plus `nosniff` and `no-referrer`. A vote is one click, and framing the chamber inside a hostile page to steal that click is the attack these shut out.

### Notes

- On-chain submission does not broadcast a transaction: that requires the delegates' voting keys and a Plutus-capable transaction builder. The rationale document, its anchor hash, and the quorum Cella reports are real.
